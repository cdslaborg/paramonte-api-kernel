\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item The benchmark results indicate that explicit if-\/blocks for optional arguments tends to be 100-\/1000 times faster than calling the convenience functions under the generic interface \mbox{\hyperlink{interfaceOption__mod_1_1genopt}{genopt}}. This performance difference tends to be about a factor of 10 times for scalar {\ttfamily optional} arguments and grows substantially to larger factors with switching to increasing-\/size array-\/like {\ttfamily optional} dummy arguments.
\item Despite significant performance degradation with the use of \mbox{\hyperlink{interfaceOption__mod_1_1genopt}{genopt}}, note that the overall average overhead of calling \mbox{\hyperlink{interfaceOption__mod_1_1genopt}{genopt}} remains extremely small as of 2022 AD, on ther order of nano to micro seconds depending on the size of the optional argument from scalar to large arrays.
\item Consequently, {\bfseries{there is practically no harm in using \mbox{\hyperlink{interfaceOption__mod_1_1genopt}{genopt}} in non-\/performance-\/critical sections of a codebase}}, that is, {\bfseries{parts of a codebase that are to be called less than billions of times at runtime}}.
