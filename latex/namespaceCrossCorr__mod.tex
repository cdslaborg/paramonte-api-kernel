Return the cross-\/correlation of the two input data vectors, (including any user-\/supplied zero padding), computed via Fast-\/\+Fourier Transform. 


\begin{DoxyParams}[1]{Parameters}
\mbox{\texttt{ in}}  & {\em padded\+Len} & \+: The lengths of the input arrays, which {\bfseries{MUST}} be an integer {\itshape power of two}. \\
\hline
\mbox{\texttt{ in}}  & {\em Padded\+Data1} & \+: The first array, possibly padded with zero to become an array of length {\ttfamily padded\+Len}. \\
\hline
\mbox{\texttt{ in}}  & {\em Padded\+Data2} & \+: The second array, possibly padded with zero to become an array of length {\ttfamily padded\+Len}.\\
\hline
\end{DoxyParams}
\begin{DoxyReturn}{Returns}
{\ttfamily Cross\+Corr\+FFT} \+: A vector of same length as the input arrays containing the cross-\/correlation. The answer is returned as the first {\ttfamily padded\+Len} points in ans stored in wrap-\/around order, i.\+e., cross-\/correlation at increasingly negative lags are in {\ttfamily Cross\+Corr\+FFT(padded\+Len)} on down to {\ttfamily Cross\+Corr\+FFT(padded\+Len/2+1)}, while cross-\/correlation increasingly positive lags are in {\ttfamily Cross\+Corr\+FFT(1)} (zero lag) on up to {\ttfamily Cross\+Corr\+FFT(padded\+Len/2)}.
\end{DoxyReturn}
\begin{DoxyRemark}{Remarks}
Sign convention of this routine\+: If {\ttfamily Padded\+Data1} lags {\ttfamily Padded\+Data2}, i.\+e., is shifted to the right of it, then ans will show a peak at positive lags.

For autocorrelation, under the assumption of a completely random series, the ACF standard error reduces to\+: $\sqrt{ 1 / \texttt{paddedLen} }$ 
\end{DoxyRemark}
