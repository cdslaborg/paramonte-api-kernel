Benchmark moral ⛓
  1. The procedures under the generic interface genCumSum are functions while the procedures under the generic interface getCumSum are subroutines. From the benchmark results, it appears that the functional interface performs less efficiently than the subroutine interface.
  2. Furthermore, specifying the array bounds on the left-hand-side (LHS) assignment in the case of the functional interface (to avoid automatic reallocation) does not appear to enhance the performance of the functional interface in any meaningful way. In other words, the compiler appears to be smart enough to not reallocate the LHS needlessly.
