Compute the break energy ebrk of the Band model, as well as the coefficient by which the high energy component of the Band model must be multiplied in order to get a smooth function (assuming the normalization coefficient of the lower energy component is unity).

Parameters
[in]epk: The spectral peak energy in units of [keV].
[in]alpha: The lower spectral exponent of the Band model.
[in]beta: The upper spectral exponent of the Band model.
[out]ebrk: The spectral break energy in units of [keV].
[out]coef: The spectral continuity coefficient.
[out]alphaPlusTwo: The lower spectral exponent of the Band model plus two.

Convert an input energy fluence in [lowerLim, upperLim] energy window, all in units of keV (or ALL in some other units), to photon fluence, within the same energy range, or if [lowerLimNew, upperLimNew] is provided, then in that range. The input tolerance is passed to integrators as a measure of the desired accuracy for the computation of photonFluence.

Parameters
[in]energyFluence: The input energy fluence (in units of [keV]) to be converted to the output photon fluence.
[in]lowerLim: The lower limit energy (in units of [keV]) of the integration.
[in]upperLim: The upper limit energy (in units of [keV]) of the integration.
[in]epk: The spectral peak energy in units of [keV].
[in]alpha: The lower spectral exponent of the Band model.
[in]beta: The upper spectral exponent of the Band model.
[in]tolerance: The relative accuracy tolerance of the integration.
[out]photonFluence: The output fluence in units of photon counts.
[out]Err: An object of class Err_type containing error-handling information.
[in]lowerLimNew: The lower limit of energy windows (in keV) to be used for the computation of photonFluence (optional).
[in]upperLimNew: The upper limit of energy windows (in keV) to be used for the computation of photonFluence (optional).
Remarks
If the optional [lowerLimNew, upperLimNew] are provided, each will replace the corresponding input [lowerLim, upperLim] in the computation of the output photonFluence.

Compute the differential photon flux according to the Band differential spectrum at the given input energy value.

Parameters
[in]energy: The energy (in units of [keV]) at which the differential photon flux must be computed.
[in]epk: The spectral peak energy in units of [keV].
[in]alpha: The lower spectral exponent of the Band model.
[in]beta: The upper spectral exponent of the Band model.
[in]ebrk: The spectral break energy in units of [keV].
[in]coef: The spectral continuity coefficient.
[in]alphaPlusTwo: The lower spectral exponent of the Band model plus two.
Returns
photonFlux : The energy flux in units of photon counts.
Warning
A negative huge output value is used to signal error has occurred. Under normal conditions, the output is always positive.
The input energy values energy, epk, ebrk, must all have the same units. It is expected that the input energy is in units of keV, although it does not affect the accuracy of the results.
